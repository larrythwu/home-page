Sumo Robot

Project Overview

This Sumo Robot was built for the Sumo Robotics Club at the University of Toronto. The project involved designing, building, and programming a robot capable of competing in sumo-style competitions where two robots attempt to push each other out of a circular ring.

Features

  • Arduino-based control system for precise movement and detection
  • 3D-printed custom chassis designed in Fusion360 for durability and optimal weight distribution
  • IR sensors for edge detection to prevent falling out of the ring
  • Ultrasonic sensors for opponent detection
  • High-torque motors for quick movements and pushing power

Technologies Used

Arduino, Fusion360 (3D modeling), C/C++ programming, Infrared and ultrasonic sensors

Challenges and Solutions

One of the main challenges was balancing speed and stability. The robot needed to be fast enough to respond quickly to opponent movements, but stable enough to provide the pushing force needed to win matches. This was solved through iterative design improvements and careful weight distribution in the chassis.
